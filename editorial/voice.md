# Overhang — Voice & Tone

> **Forked from Drift's editorial memory on 2026-06-20** as a starting point. Keep the DNA; let Overhang develop its own idioms and closing-mode log over time. When this diverges meaningfully from Drift, that's expected and good.

Until Overhang's overture post (`the-overhang`) exists, use Drift's **"Everything Drifts"** as the reference voice. Once the overture is written, it becomes Overhang's own reference post. Key qualities:

- First-person throughout ("I", "my", "I've found")
- Self-aware, occasionally wry/self-deprecating
- Admits uncertainty explicitly — but vary *how* (see closing taxonomy below)
- Personal stakes: the author has lived or watched the thing they're writing about, not just researched it
- Short declarative sentences after longer analytical ones for rhythm

**Why:** Technical/finance posts drift toward white-paper / sell-side-research tone with no authorial voice. The voice is the differentiator.

**How to apply:** Check for first-person presence; ground major sections in at least one specific observation or anecdote; don't bold every statistic — reserve bold for truly pivotal figures. (Overhang will be number-heavy; this restraint matters more here than it did for Drift.)

## Closing mode taxonomy

Posts should end on honest uncertainty or open tension — but the *form* should rotate. Track which mode each post uses in the log below.

| # | Mode | Description | Marker |
|---|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **Unresolved question** | Pose a specific question the post has earned the right not to answer | Ends with "?" |
| 2 | **Honest inventory** | What this post established vs. what remains open — stated plainly, no question mark | "What I think I know: X. What I don't: Y." |
| 3 | **The provocation** | A statement the post earns the right to make — slightly uncomfortable, grounded, stated plainly | Short declarative final sentence with edge |
| 4 | **Personal stake** | Close with what *you* plan to watch or do differently — shifts from analysis to implication | "What I'm watching is..." |
| 5 | **The kicker** | One sentence that reframes the whole post — crystallizes what it adds up to | 1–2 sentence final paragraph that snaps the post into focus |

**Anti-patterns:**
- Don't use mode 1 in consecutive posts.
- Don't repeat a confession phrase verbatim across posts — once it recurs, it's a tell.
- Don't reuse a section heading as a closing crutch.

## Deferred reward prompt (required at publish — not optional)

A required step in the pre-publish checklist (see `workflow.md`). Don't end a publish session without asking it and logging the response.

> **Closing check — [post slug]**
> Closing mode used: [mode name]
> Last 2 sentences: "[excerpt]"
>
> How did this land?
> (a) Felt fresh and earned
> (b) Technically fine but not memorable
